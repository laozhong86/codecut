# Cover Archetypes

Choose one archetype before writing the prompt. The archetype controls visual
composition only; Codecut still imports and sets a normal project cover.

## `xhs-atutun-human-title`

Use for Xiaohongshu-style AI tools, tutorials, product reviews, productivity
methods, personal experience, podcast/resource recommendations, and beginner
education.

- Ratio: `3:4`.
- Evidence: primary human emotion frame plus optional product or UI proof frame.
- Visual: human cutout, strong expression, huge Chinese title, black outline,
  `#FDFFA7` or white text, green checklist, stickers, arrows, high density.
- Prompt rule: preserve the person from the primary frame; use support frames as
  product, interface, or atmosphere references.
- Title rule: main title should be 4-10 Chinese characters when possible.

## `short-video-emotion-poster`

Use for TikTok, Douyin, Reels, Shorts, and WeChat Channels when the cover must
sell a vertical short.

- Ratio: usually `9:16`.
- Evidence: emotion or action frame with one readable subject.
- Visual: one dominant subject, one hook, enough title-safe negative space,
  platform-safe bottom area, punchy but not cluttered.
- Prompt rule: do not add dense small text; use one short title or no baked text
  when the platform/editor will add title separately.

## `long-video-thumbnail`

Use for YouTube, Bilibili, courses, podcasts, explainers, and horizontal videos.

- Ratio: usually `16:9`.
- Evidence: expressive face, strong product/result frame, or clear conflict.
- Visual: one large subject or face, short high-contrast title, simple
  background, strong separation.
- Prompt rule: keep the title readable at small size and avoid thin fonts.

## `tutorial-screen-proof`

Use for screen recordings, software demos, deployment tutorials, AI-tool
walkthroughs, and workflow explanations.

- Ratio: platform-dependent.
- Evidence: screen-state or result-proof frame.
- Visual: enlarged interface state, clear before/after or result cue, one title
  block, cursor/arrow only when useful.
- Prompt rule: do not invent app screens, charts, or UI content.

## `product-proof-cover`

Use for product demos, reviews, commerce proof, comparison, and before/after.

- Ratio: platform-dependent.
- Evidence: product detail, usage, result, packaging, or comparison proof frame.
- Visual: product large and inspectable, one proof cue, clean contrast, title
  space that does not cover labels or usage evidence.
- Prompt rule: do not create unsupported efficacy, price, guarantee, or official
  endorsement claims.

## `cinematic-mood-cover`

Use for story, travel, documentary, local life, emotional narrative, and premium
brand clips.

- Ratio: platform-dependent.
- Evidence: atmosphere frame with character, place, or story question.
- Visual: cinematic but realistic lighting, readable silhouette, restrained
  title, strong mood.
- Prompt rule: avoid fake movie credits, poster collage, and unrelated props.
