# Short Video Strategy Example

## PlatformStrategyBrief

This brief does not mutate the timeline. It is upstream of a CodeCut `EditingDecisionLedger`.

## Context

- Platform: Xiaohongshu
- Business outcome: save/search discovery for a skincare product review
- Audience: users comparing whether the product fits sensitive skin
- Source format: vertical creator review with product close-ups
- Source freshness / current-source check: no latest platform-rule claim; platform guidance treated as dated hypothesis
- Algorithm stance: use search fit, trust, and save value as operating hypotheses

## Evidence

- Transcript evidence available: creator explains texture and usage order
- Visual proof available: product texture close-up and before/after skin lighting
- Product or claim evidence available: no clinical efficacy evidence
- Analytics, comments, or search evidence available: missing
- Evidence missing: no price, ingredient proof, or claim substantiation

## Signal Priorities

1. Search fit: title, first line, and caption should align around the same user query.
2. Trust/proof: show texture and real-use result before recommendation language.
3. Save value: preserve comparison details and usage steps.

## Editing Strategy

- Editing bias: useful note, not hard-sell ad.
- Hook candidates: "Sensitive skin texture test", "I used it for 7 days", "What surprised me is the finish".
- Recommended structure: problem -> texture proof -> usage step -> visible result -> save-worthy summary.
- Caption / overlay direction: clean labels, ingredient or usage notes only where evidence exists.
- CTA or loop: soft save cue, no stacked buy/follow/comment commands.
- Safe-zone notes: preserve product and face center; avoid dense bottom overlays.

## CodeCut Handoff

- `materialAudit`: check transcript for claims; inspect texture and before/after shots.
- `storyBeats`: problem, product texture, usage, result, limitation.
- `candidateClips`: texture close-up, before/after, creator credibility line.
- `selectedStructure`: Xiaohongshu search/save trust structure.
- `qaChecklist`: no unsupported efficacy claim; cover/title aligns with first frame; CTA stays soft.

## Risks

- Unsupported platform claim: none; no current algorithm claim made.
- Unsupported product claim: efficacy language needs evidence.
- Platform mismatch: hard TikTok CTA would weaken trust.
- Confidence: medium until transcript and visual proof are inspected.
