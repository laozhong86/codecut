# WeChat Channels

Use this as a China-market dated hypothesis. Run a current-source check for latest WeChat Channels creator, recommendation, social sharing, commerce, or policy guidance. Do not claim secret ranking weights.

## Editing bias

WeChat Channels often benefits from social trust, shareability, credible explanation, and relationship-context distribution. The edit can be slightly calmer than TikTok if the content earns trust and makes the reason to forward clear.

Good structures:

- Credible problem -> useful explanation -> proof -> share-worthy takeaway.
- Human story -> lesson -> product or service relevance -> soft action.
- Local or community context -> evidence -> clear next step.

## Content strategy

- `audienceIntent`: learn, trust, forward to someone, consult, or understand a practical issue.
- `contentAngle`: credible explainer, founder story, community case, local context, or useful warning.
- `scriptPromise`: one practical takeaway worth forwarding.
- `proofAsset`: professional context, real case, original footage, service scene, customer question, or article/account credibility.
- `interactionDesign`: share, consult, follow, group discussion, or save for later.
- `editingImplication`: keep enough context to build trust, make the share reason explicit, and avoid excessive jump-cut pressure.

## Evidence to inspect

- Creator credibility, professional context, or relationship trust cues.
- Whether a viewer would forward it to a friend, client, parent, or group.
- Clear spoken explanation and readable captions.
- Visual evidence that supports claims without over-stimulation.
- Whether the CTA fits WeChat context: follow, consult, save, or share.

## Avoid

- Excessive jump cuts that damage trust.
- Overly loud hard-sell pacing copied from Douyin ads.
- Burying the practical takeaway after a long intro.
- Unsupported authority, endorsement, or official claims.
- Ignoring social sharing context when selecting the opening beat.
