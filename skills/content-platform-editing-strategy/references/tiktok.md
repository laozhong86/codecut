# TikTok

Public TikTok materials describe recommendation as personalized ranking based on user interactions, video information, device/account settings, and feedback. Treat creative guidance as a dated hypothesis and run a current-source check for latest TikTok Shop, policy, or monetization rules. Do not claim secret ranking weights.

## Editing bias

TikTok editing should usually maximize immediate clarity, native feel, rewatch potential, and proof density. Strong edits open on the payoff or tension, then quickly prove why the viewer should stay.

Good structures:

- Pattern break -> proof/demo -> value beat -> CTA or loop.
- Creator confession -> evidence -> result -> comment-worthy question.
- Product problem -> real usage -> before/after -> one action.

## Content strategy

- `audienceIntent`: satisfy curiosity, solve a visible pain, compare options, verify a product, or act on a creator recommendation.
- `contentAngle`: pattern break, result-first, problem-solution, creator confession, comparison, or challenge.
- `scriptPromise`: a fast, native-feeling reason to stay for the proof.
- `proofAsset`: demo, before/after, order page, packaging, result, creator reaction, or side-by-side comparison.
- `interactionDesign`: watch-through, rewatch, comment, share/send, profile visit, or TikTok Shop action.
- `editingImplication`: first frame must create a question or show proof; proof should outrank explanation when both compete.

## Evidence to inspect

- First 1-3 seconds: visual payoff, spoken hook, and setup delay.
- Watch-time blockers: dead air, redundant setup, unclear subject, weak audio.
- Proof shots: demo, comparison, customer context, order/packaging, result.
- Rewatch loop: reveal timing, before/after, callback ending.
- Native cues: creator voice, comments, trend fit, and direct product relevance.

## Avoid

- Corporate intro cards before the first proof.
- Hashtag or trend stuffing with no viewer value.
- Engagement bait that does not relate to the content.
- Unsupported TikTok Shop performance or policy claims.
- Hiding weak evidence behind fast captions.
